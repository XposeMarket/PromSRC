# Copywriting Formulas

## The Big 5

### 1. AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
Classic for ads, emails, landing pages.
- **Attention**: Grab with a bold headline or hook
- **Interest**: Build curiosity or relevance ("Here's why this matters to you")
- **Desire**: Paint the outcome / benefit / transformation
- **Action**: One clear CTA

### 2. PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution
Best for landing pages and email openers.
- **Problem**: Name the pain clearly ("If you're struggling with X...")
- **Agitation**: Make it feel worse ("And every day you wait, Y happens...")
- **Solution**: Introduce your product as the relief

### 3. BAB — Before, After, Bridge
Great for testimonials and case studies.
- **Before**: Describe life before your product
- **After**: Describe life after your product
- **Bridge**: Your product is the bridge between the two

### 4. FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits
For product descriptions and feature announcements.
- **Feature**: What it does ("Automated scheduling")
- **Advantage**: Why that matters ("Eliminates back-and-forth emails")
- **Benefit**: What the customer gets ("Get 3 hours back every week")
Always lead with Benefit in headlines, use Feature as support.

### 5. 4 U's — Urgent, Unique, Useful, Ultra-specific
Use to grade any headline. Strong headlines score 3-4 out of 4.
- Urgent: Creates time pressure
- Unique: Different from what they've seen
- Useful: Delivers clear value
- Ultra-specific: Uses numbers, names, dates ("In 17 minutes", "For SaaS founders", "by Q2")

## Power Headlines Formulas

```
"How to [achieve desired result] without [dreaded thing]"
"The [number] [things] every [ICP] needs to know about [topic]"
"Why [counterintuitive claim] — and what to do about it"
"[Specific result] in [timeframe]: Here's exactly how"
"Stop [wrong behavior]. Do this instead."
"The [adjective] truth about [topic] that nobody talks about"
"[Famous person / category leader] does X. Here's why you should too."
"[Number] [ICP] reveal their [secret/mistake/shortcut] for [result]"
```

## Email Subject Line Formulas

```
Curiosity: "I probably shouldn't tell you this..."
Personalization: "[First name], quick question"
Specificity: "8 minutes to [result]"
Social proof: "How [Company] got [result] in [timeframe]"
Direct benefit: "Your [metric] is about to improve"
Contrarian: "Why [common advice] is wrong"
FOMO: "Last chance — [offer] ends tonight"
Re-engagement: "Is this still relevant to you?"
```

## CTA Copy That Converts

Avoid: "Submit", "Click here", "Learn more"
Use instead:
- "Get my free [thing]" (possessive + specific)
- "Start saving time today" (outcome-oriented)
- "Show me how it works" (curious, low-commitment)
- "Yes, I want [specific result]" (affirming)
- "Book my free strategy call" (possessive + value)
- "Get instant access" (immediacy)
- "Try it free for 14 days" (risk reversal)
- "See [Company]'s results" (social proof)

## Objection Handlers

Common objections and how to handle in copy:

**"It's too expensive"**
→ Reframe to ROI: "At $X/month, [Product] pays for itself when you [outcome worth more than $X]"
→ Compare to cost of not solving: "How much is [problem] costing you right now?"

**"I don't have time to set this up"**
→ "Most customers are up and running in under 20 minutes"
→ "Our onboarding team does the setup for you"

**"I'm not sure it'll work for my situation"**
→ Use specific use cases: "Whether you're a [type A] or [type B]..."
→ Risk reversal: "Try it free. If it doesn't work, you pay nothing."

**"I need to think about it"**
→ Urgency: "This offer expires [date]"
→ Social proof: "Join 10,000 others who've already made the switch"

**"I've tried similar things before"**
→ Differentiation: "Unlike [category], [Product] actually [key differentiator]"
→ Testimonial from someone who felt the same way
