# Polsia Reference → Prometheus Adaptation

Use this as inspiration, not imitation. The useful direction is not the specific company visuals; it is the feeling that the viewer has found evidence of an autonomous system already operating.

## What to adapt

- Archival, industrial, documentary, scientific-analysis, and internal-system aesthetics.
- Real/process proof: tool traces, file paths, browser/desktop captures, task rows, memory cards, approvals, generated artifacts.
- Pale dossier UI: off-white canvas, serif headings, thin rules, document panels, small status dots, bureaucratic calm.
- Black negative-space identity moments: tiny warm line mark or ember glyph centered in darkness.
- Monochrome or near-monochrome frames with one controlled status accent.
- Analog mediation: subtle scanlines, photocopy grain, screen capture softness, printer marks, old laboratory/field-manual texture.
- Cinematic captions and short declarations that feel like documentary evidence.

## What not to copy

- The exact Polsia face mark, UI, copy, investor-launch structure, or testimonials.
- Excessive fake scientific labels that do not map to real Prometheus concepts.
- Generic glitch/cyberpunk overload.
- Purple/blue/cyan gradient SaaS language unless brand-required.
- Abstract tech darkness without concrete Prometheus proof.

## Prometheus translation

Polsia's direction: futuristic AI company that builds companies.

Prometheus direction: local intelligence command center that grows into the user's life and work.

Make Prometheus feel local, personal, mythic, desktop-native, proof-driven, and operational.
