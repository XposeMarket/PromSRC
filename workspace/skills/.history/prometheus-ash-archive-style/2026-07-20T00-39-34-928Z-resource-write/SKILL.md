# Prometheus Ash & Archive Style

This is explicit-only. Never impose it on unrelated creative work.

## Direction

Prometheus should feel like an ancient intelligence operating through a modern local command center: industrial field manual, archival control room, and living desktop OS. Show real process evidence—files, local routes, tools, tasks, memory, approvals, teams, schedules, artifacts—not decorative pseudo-HUD labels.

Use the bundled [Ash & Archive palette](palettes/ash-archive.md): obsidian, bone, charcoal, ember, oxblood, machine green, and rare brass. Avoid default purple/blue/cyan AI gradients, generic glass dashboards, chatbot bubbles, cyberpunk cosplay, and rounded panels around everything.

Use three typographic voices: editorial serif for authority, monospace for machine traces, and a serious readable face for documents. Add paper grain, printer marks, scanlines, stamps, rules, and degradation sparingly enough to preserve legibility.

Motion should reveal structure: document slides, mechanical wipes, stamps, log ticks, row activation, measured evidence pans, decisive cuts, and a restrained ember finish. Do not make every object float or glow.

For the full visual thesis, promo structure, copy tone, and QA rubric, read [the detailed guide](references/detailed-guide.md). For HyperFrames, also use `hyperframes`, `hyperframes-creative`, and the [house-style bridge](../hyperframes-creative/references/house-style.md).

Before delivery, render/export, inspect representative frames, verify readable text and real process proof, and confirm the aesthetic was explicitly requested.
