# Cold outreach

Write brief, relevant messages that earn a reply without pretending to know more than the evidence supports.

1. Confirm recipient, channel, relationship, verified facts, offer, proof, desired next step, and voice.
2. Choose one relevant observation and one defensible problem/value hypothesis.
3. Draft a specific opening, concise value bridge, credible proof, and low-friction CTA.
4. Produce variants that change angle—not only synonyms.
5. Check factual accuracy, personalization, tone, length, deliverability risks, and consent/policy constraints.

Never fabricate research, mutual connections, results, urgency, or familiarity. Avoid fake “quick question” framing, guilt, and mass-message language. Do not send without explicit authorization.
