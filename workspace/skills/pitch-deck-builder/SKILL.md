# Pitch Deck Builder

Build pitch decks that tell a tight story, earn trust, and move investors to a next meeting.

---

## The Core Principle

A pitch deck is not a product brochure. It's a story about the future.

The investor question you're answering: **"Is this team, attacking this market, at this time, with this approach — worth a bet?"**

Every slide either builds or destroys the answer to that question.

---

## Step 1: Pre-Deck Discovery

Ask these before writing a single slide:

```
1. Stage: Pre-seed / Seed / Series A / Series B+?
2. Amount raising: $___
3. Use of funds: [3 specific things the money buys]
4. Traction to date: Revenue? Users? MoM growth? Retention?
5. The one-liner: What does [Company] do in one sentence?
6. The insight: What do you know that most people don't?
7. The market: How big? How do you know?
8. The team: Why are you the people to win this?
9. Format: Narrative deck (story-led) or data room deck (evidence-led)?
10. Audience: Generalist VC / sector-specific / angels / strategic?
```

---

## Step 2: Standard Deck Structure (12 slides)

### Slide 1 — Cover
```
Company name + one-liner + logo
Optional: one striking visual or data point
```
**Rule:** The one-liner must make the investor curious, not explain everything.

---

### Slide 2 — Problem
```
What's broken in the world right now?
For whom? How painful? How frequent?
Optional: the "before" state — a day in the life
```
**Rule:** Make the investor feel the pain before you show the solution. No jargon.

---

### Slide 3 — Solution
```
What you do. One sentence.
How it solves the problem. Mechanism, not features.
Optional: product screenshot, demo GIF, or visual
```
**Rule:** Don't list features. Show the transformation: before → after.

---

### Slide 4 — Why Now
```
What has changed in the world that makes this possible and necessary today?
Technology shift / regulatory change / behavioral shift / infrastructure unlock
```
**Rule:** This is often skipped. Don't skip it. Timing is half the investment thesis.

---

### Slide 5 — Market Size
```
TAM / SAM / SOM with methodology shown
Bottom-up is more credible than top-down for early stage
```
**Rule:** Don't say "$50B market" without showing how you got there. Show the math.

```
Bottom-up example:
  10M companies in the US use [category]
  We target SMBs: ~3M companies
  Average contract value: $2,400/yr
  Addressable: $7.2B SAM
```

---

### Slide 6 — Product
```
3-5 screenshots or workflow GIF
Focus on the "magic moment" — the thing that makes users say "whoa"
```
**Rule:** Show, don't describe. Less copy, more product.

---

### Slide 7 — Traction
```
The most impressive metrics you have, honestly presented
Revenue, users, growth rate, retention, NPS, logo quality
Time series > snapshots
```
**Rule:** Include the Y-axis and dates. Investors have seen cropped hockey sticks.

```
Strong traction framing:
  "From $0 to $45K MRR in 8 months"
  "65% of users return weekly (vs. 23% category average)"
  "3 of our 12 customers are Fortune 500s"
```

---

### Slide 8 — Business Model
```
How you make money
Unit economics if available (CAC, LTV, payback period)
Pricing model: per seat / usage / flat / marketplace
```
**Rule:** Show one or two numbers that prove the model works at scale.

---

### Slide 9 — Go-to-Market
```
How you acquire customers
Channel, motion (PLG/sales-led/channel), and ICP
First 100 customers: how did you get them?
Next 1000: what changes?
```
**Rule:** Be specific. "Content marketing and partnerships" is not a GTM strategy.

---

### Slide 10 — Competition
```
Landscape map or table
Your position and moat
```
**Rule:** Don't say "no real competition." Pick the right axes that make your differentiation obvious.

---

### Slide 11 — Team
```
Founders + key hires
Relevant prior experience (logos matter)
Why this team for this market specifically
```
**Rule:** Lead with the "unfair advantage" — domain expertise, prior exit, distribution built-in.

---

### Slide 12 — The Ask
```
Amount: $___
Use of funds: [3 buckets with % allocation]
Milestones this round gets you to
```
**Rule:** Be specific. "Hire engineers and grow sales" is not an answer. Map spend to outcomes.

---

## Step 3: Narrative Arc

The best decks follow a story arc, not a data dump:

```
ACT 1 — THE WORLD (slides 1-4)
  Setup: something is broken
  Why it exists: structural cause
  Why now: the timing unlock

ACT 2 — THE OPPORTUNITY (slides 5-7)
  The market: how big is the prize
  Our bet: what we're building
  Proof: evidence it's working

ACT 3 — THE COMPANY (slides 8-11)
  How it works: model and GTM
  Why we win: moat, competition, team

ACT 4 — THE ASK (slide 12)
  The specific bet we're asking you to make
```

---

## Slide-Level Quality Checks

For every slide:
- [ ] One main idea per slide (if you need a comma, split it)
- [ ] Can be understood in 10 seconds by a distracted person
- [ ] Has a headline that states the conclusion, not just the topic
- [ ] No more than 40 words of body copy
- [ ] Every claim has a source or a caveat

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| "We have no competition" | Pick better comparison axes |
| Vague market size ($1T market!) | Show bottom-up math |
| Feature list instead of transformation | Show before/after |
| No Y-axis on growth charts | Always label axes with real numbers |
| Team slide with just names/titles | Add the "why this team" narrative |
| Missing Why Now slide | Add it — timing is the thesis |
| Asking for too-round a number | "$3.2M" is more credible than "$3M" |
| 25+ slides | Cut to 12-15 max for narrative deck |

---

## Deck Variants by Stage

```
Pre-seed (idea / early prototype):
  Lead with team, insight, and market
  Traction = early customer conversations, not revenue
  12 slides, story-heavy

Seed ($500K-$3M):
  Lead with traction and momentum
  Product screenshots are critical
  Unit economics are a plus but not required

Series A ($3M-$15M):
  Revenue + growth rate are the story
  Unit economics required (esp. CAC/LTV)
  GTM motion must be proven, not hypothetical
  Team: not just founders — key hires that de-risk execution
```

---

## Final Output Format

Produce slide-by-slide content as markdown with:
- Slide number and name
- Headline (the conclusion statement)
- Body copy (under 40 words)
- Visual direction note (what image/chart/screenshot belongs here)
- Speaker notes (what to say verbally that's NOT on the slide)
