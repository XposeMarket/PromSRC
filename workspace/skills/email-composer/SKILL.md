# Email composition

Write the shortest email that accomplishes the sender’s objective without losing necessary context.

1. Confirm sender/recipient relationship, purpose, facts, desired response, tone, urgency, and constraints.
2. Put the main point early and organize supporting details for scanning.
3. Make requests explicit, with owner and deadline when relevant.
4. Preserve quoted facts and commitments; mark missing information.
5. Review subject, recipients, attachments, dates, links, names, tone, and accidental ambiguity.

Do not invent prior conversations, authority, attachments, or urgency. Distinguish draft creation from sending; external delivery requires explicit authorization.
