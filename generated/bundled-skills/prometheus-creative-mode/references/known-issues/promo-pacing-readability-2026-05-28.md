# Prometheus Promo Pacing / Readability Guardrail — 2026-05-28

## Trigger
Use this note when creating Prometheus, Xpose Market, HyperFrames, Creative Mode, launch, promo, explainer, or product-demo clips with substantial on-screen text.

## Evidence
- Raul liked the 18s Ash & Archive HyperFrames `hyperframes-composite-tools` explainer, but corrected that the scenes transitioned too quickly and people could not read the text fast enough.
- Research later that day reinforced a better production lane: voiceover-first pacing, transcript-derived captions, separate audio tracks, avatar/source video as media layers, and visuals that breathe instead of racing.

## Guardrail
Do not optimize Prometheus promo/explainer clips for maximum cut density. Default to readable pacing:

- Use fewer, stronger scenes with longer holds instead of many rapid cuts.
- Keep key title/stat/product text visible long enough to read aloud comfortably.
- Prefer voiceover-first timing for explainers, then sync visuals/captions to the narration.
- Use captions in short 2–6 word groups when voiceover is present.
- Avoid stacking multiple new concepts, UI movements, and large text blocks in the same sub-second beat.
- For dense product ideas, let the visual hold while motion happens around it: scanning lines, subtle parallax, stamp/reveal, cursor movement, glow pulse, progress bar, or dashboard change.
- QA at representative frames and, when possible, watch/scrub the actual export or contact sheet for reading time before claiming success.

## Practical defaults
- Short social bumper with text-only scenes: ~2.0–3.5s per major text beat.
- Explainer scenes: ~4–7s per concept when there is no voiceover; pace to narration when there is.
- Big stat/claim reveal: keep the final value/result visible for at least ~1.5–2s.
- Outro/CTA: hold long enough to read once without pausing.

## Exception
If Raul explicitly asks for frantic/glitch/trailer pacing, use the faster style — but keep critical claims/captions readable or repeat them in a slower final hold.
