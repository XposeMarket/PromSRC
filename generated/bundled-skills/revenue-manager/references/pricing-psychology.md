# Pricing Psychology & Tactics

## Anchoring
Always show a higher price first. The first number seen becomes the reference point.
- Show annual price before monthly
- Show highest tier first (right to left display)
- Show "original price" crossed out before sale price
- Show competitor price before your price

## Charm Pricing
- $97 outperforms $100 (left-digit effect)
- $9.99 feels like $9, not $10
- Exception: luxury goods should use round numbers ($500, not $499) — round numbers signal quality

## The Decoy Effect
Add a third option that makes your preferred option look like the obvious choice.
Example:
- Small: $5 (low anchor)
- Medium: $8 ← decoy (bad value vs. large)  
- Large: $9 ← target (feels like a steal vs. medium)

## Bundle Pricing
- Bundling hides the per-unit price and reduces price sensitivity
- "Everything included" feels like value even if rarely used
- Good/Better/Best bundles increase AOV vs. à la carte
- Add a "most popular" badge to the bundle you want to sell

## Scarcity & Urgency
- "Only 3 left" increases conversion 15-30%
- "Price increases in 48 hours" works but must be true (customers remember)
- "X people looking at this right now" (social proof + scarcity)

## Payment Framing
- "$1/day" feels cheaper than "$365/year" — same price
- "Less than your daily coffee" anchors to trivial spend
- Monthly billing feels cheaper than annual (even if annual is cheaper total)
- Free trials remove purchase anxiety — 30% convert if card required, 15% without

## Price Increase Messaging
Bad: "We're raising prices"
Good: "We're investing in [specific improvement], and our pricing will reflect this"
Better: Grandfather loyal customers for 3-6 months — they become advocates not detractors

## Willingness to Pay Signals
- No one haggles → underpriced
- 20% churn on price → sweet spot
- >50% mention price as objection → overpriced OR under-communicated value
- Best signal: Van Westendorp survey (at what price is it too cheap? too expensive? acceptable?)

## Industry Benchmark Pricing Margins
| Industry | Typical Gross Margin | Healthy Net Margin |
|---|---|---|
| SaaS | 70-85% | 15-25% (at scale) |
| E-commerce | 30-50% | 5-15% |
| Hotels | 55-70% (RevPAR margin) | 10-20% |
| Agencies | 40-60% | 15-25% |
| Marketplaces | 60-80% (take rate) | 10-20% |
| Manufacturing | 25-45% | 5-15% |
