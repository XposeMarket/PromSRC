# Ghostwriting

Reproduce communicative patterns without fabricating identity or experience.

1. Gather audience, purpose, format, source facts, voice samples, phrases to avoid, and approval boundary.
2. Extract observable voice features: sentence rhythm, vocabulary, humor, directness, structure, stance, and typical proof.
3. Draft from verified ideas and experiences only.
4. Compare the draft against voice features and remove generic filler or borrowed mannerisms that do not fit.
5. Mark factual gaps and language that requires the owner’s confirmation.

Do not invent memories, clients, results, beliefs, or quotes. Do not use voice matching for fraud or misleading impersonation.
